## What this does

Dust collects on fans, heatsinks, and intake filters and acts like insulation, trapping heat. That raises CPU and GPU temperatures, and hot hardware **thermal throttles** - it slows itself down to stay safe.

## Expected benefit

- This is one of the few manual jobs with a real, measurable payoff on a dusty PC
- Temperature drops of 5-15 C are common on a neglected machine, which directly removes throttling
- On an already-clean PC, there is nothing to gain

## Prerequisites

- A can of compressed air, or an electric air duster
- Ideally a soft brush
- Somewhere with airflow - a garage or outdoors is ideal

## Steps

1. Shut down the PC and unplug the power cable.
2. Press the power button once to discharge any remaining power.
3. Open the side panel.
4. Blow dust off the GPU heatsink and fans, the CPU cooler, the case fans, and the power supply intake.
5. **Hold each fan still with a finger while you blow it.** Spinning a fan with compressed air can damage it.
6. Remove the dust filters, then rinse or blow them clean. Let washed filters dry **completely**.
7. Refit the filters and close the case.

## How to verify it worked

- Heatsinks and filters are visibly clean.
- If you want a number, check CPU and GPU temperatures with HWiNFO before and after.

## How to revert

- Nothing to revert - removing dust has no downside.

## Common pitfalls

- Always hold fans still while blowing them. A free-spinning fan generates voltage that can harm the fan or the board.
- Do not use a household vacuum cleaner - it can build up static electricity.
- Compressed-air cans get very cold and can spit freezing liquid if tilted. Keep the can upright.
- Refit dust filters only once they are fully dry.
